The process of vacciantion

The vaccine is a two-component, it is injected into the human body twice. The second vaccination is
done three weeks after the first. The drug is administered intramuscularly, in a dose of 0.5 ml.
After the procedure, the patient must remain under medical supervision for at least 30 minutes to report on
their condition, and then keep a self-observation diary.
It is important that before vaccination, the patient is examined for the presence of contraindications to vaccination.
A mandatory medical examination is carried out. He will tell you about possible reactions and help you fill out
an informational voluntary consent for vaccination.
Immunization will take place in three stages. First of all, the vaccine will be given to those people
who are at risk of covid infection (doctors working in the " red " zone, PCR laboratories, emergency rooms, in the ambulance,
teachers, volunteers, MFC employees, social workers).
At the second stage, people suffering from chronic diseases, heart disease, diseases of the gastrointestinal tract,
and patients who are under constant dispensary supervision will receive the vaccination.
The third stage includes vaccination of emergency workers, law enforcement officers, and ordinary citizens.

Who can be vaccinated?

Patients with coronavirus can be immunized no earlier than 6 months after recovery. If a person has beaten
the coronavirus in the last six months, their body is checked for the amount of antibodies.
If the indicator is below ten, the patient can be vaccinated.
Above ten is a sufficient number of antibodies to defeat the coronavirus when re-infected.
The instructions for vaccination do not contain the need for testing for coronavirus before immunization.
